ORCA: Objects, Relationships, Calls to Action

OOUX (Object-Oriented UX) is a design methodology for modeling systems around real-world objects rather than screens or flows. ORCA — Objects, Relationships, Calls to Action — is the core framework of OOUX. This reference applies ORCA to the Safety Net Blueprint: an object-by-object guide to what data exists in the system, how objects relate to each other, and what actions can be taken on them.

Data Domains

The data model is organized into these domains:

  • Client Management — Persistent client identity & history
  • Intake — Application submission & intake processing
  • Eligibility — Program eligibility determination
  • Case Management — Cases, workers & staff assignments
  • Document Management — Files, uploads & verification
  • Workflow — Task lifecycle, queues & SLA tracking
  • Data Exchange — External service facade — FDSH, IEVS, SAVE, SSA
  • Scheduling — Appointments & interviews
  • Communications — Notices, letters & client alerts
  • Appeals & Hearings — Fair hearings & appeal decisions
  • Benefits & Payments — Benefit issuance, EBT & recoupment

Application

What is Application?

The root entity representing one submitted application from a household. Distinct from the downstream benefit case — no platform tracks the final determination on the application itself; that lives on the case created after eligibility determination. Drives the regulatory processing clock: the 30-day SNAP deadline and 45-day Medicaid deadline both run from submittedAt (7 CFR § 273.2, 42 CFR § 435.912).

SNAP requires all household members to be listed regardless of individual eligibility (7 CFR § 273.1).

Verification

What is Verification?

One item on the household's verification checklist, covering both electronic service check results and paper document requirements on a single lifecycle. Created automatically by state machine event handlers at submission — not manually by caseworkers. The two-phase flow (electronic check first, document request only if inconclusive) directly encodes the ex parte rule (42 CFR § 435.940).
